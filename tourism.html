Popular Tourist Places

Explore Kadapa's most iconic destinations

Nature · Heritage
Gandikota Fort & Gorge
Jammalamadugu, 72km from Kadapa

Known as the "Grand Canyon of India", Gandikota gorge on the Penna River is one of India's most stunning natural spectacles, combined with a 14th century fort.

Best: Oct–Mar Full Day
Pilgrimage
Vontimitta Kodandarama Temple
Vontimitta, 45km from Kadapa

One of the most magnificent Dravidian-style temples in Andhra Pradesh, believed to be the spot where Lord Rama halted during the Ramayana journey.

All Year Half Day
Adventure · Nature
Belum Caves
Near Kolimigundla, 90km from Kadapa

The second-largest natural cave system in India, with over 3.5km of underground passages, stalactites, and ancient Buddhist ruins.

Oct–Feb 3–4 Hours
Pilgrimage · Trekking
Pushpagiri Temple
Pushpagiri, Kadapa Dist.

A hilltop Shaivite pilgrimage center with scenic views. The trek through dense forests to the hilltop temple makes it an adventure-spiritual combo.

Oct–Feb Trekking
Heritage · History
Siddhavattam Fort
Siddavatam, 55km from Kadapa

An ancient fort on the banks of Penna River, once ruled by Qutb Shahi sultans. The fort-temple complex offers a glimpse of medieval Deccan architecture.

All Year Photography
Nature · Scenic
Sunkesula Gorge
Kurnool border, Kadapa Dist.

A stunning gorge on the Tungabhadra river with a hanging bridge and a centuries-old shrine. Popular for its crocodile sanctuary and scenic beauty.

Oct–Jan Wildlife

Travel Information

By Air

Nearest airport is Tirupati Airport (~180km). Hyderabad (RGIA) is another option (~370km). Pre-booked taxis are available.

By Train

Kadapa Railway Station (code: HX) is on the Secunderabad–Chennai main line. Direct trains from Hyderabad, Bengaluru, and Chennai.

By Road

Kadapa is well connected via NH40 and NH340. APSRTC buses run frequently from Hyderabad (6h), Bengaluru (5h), and Chennai (6h).
